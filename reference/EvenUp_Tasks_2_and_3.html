Reporting Framework: Channel Efficiency & Pipeline Concentration

Designed to help leadership monitor, rebalance, and optimize the marketing channel mix based on pipeline contribution and efficiency ‚Äî not just activity volume.

Why This Insight?

The Q1 data reveals a critical structural risk: 68% of all pipeline ($18.8M of $27.6M) comes from a single channel ‚Äî Hosted Events. Meanwhile, the highest-volume channel (Direct Mail at 36% of touches) generates only 11% of pipeline. This imbalance means a single disruption to the events program (budget cuts, travel restrictions, event cancellations) would wipe out the majority of pipeline generation overnight.

Leadership needs a framework to continuously monitor this concentration, identify when channels are over- or under-performing relative to investment, and make informed rebalancing decisions before problems hit the pipeline.

The data shows "Attended" contacts convert at 27.1% meeting rate and generate $30,593 pipeline per touch ‚Äî the strongest engagement signal in the entire dataset. Yet 73% of attendees (392 people) left events without a booked meeting and received no structured follow-up.

This isn't a lead quality problem ‚Äî these are Prospects (218), existing Customers (140), and even Churned accounts (34) who showed up in person. Industry research supports this: a significant share of trade show leads never receive effective follow-up, with industry surveys reporting that roughly 64% of event leads aren't properly followed up on.

‚ùå Current State

  • No standardized post-event follow-up process
  • AEs own follow-up but no SLA or accountability
  • No segmentation by engagement level (Attended vs Badge Scan vs No Show)
  • No automated nurture for attendees who don't book immediately
  • Gifting program (701 gifts delivered) operates without follow-up triggers
  • No recycling ‚Äî leads that don't convert are abandoned

‚úÖ Proposed State

  • Tiered follow-up SLA based on engagement depth + account tier
  • Auto-created SFDC tasks with due dates and escalation paths
  • Engagement-based segmentation drives routing priority
  • Automated Marketo nurture for attendees not booked within 72 hours
  • Gift delivery triggers mandatory AE outreach task within 48 hours
  • 14-day recycle rule: unworked leads return to marketing nurture with reason code

The Solution: Tiered Post-Event Engagement SLA

Step 1: Segment Attendees by Priority

Priority Tier Criteria AE SLA Automation Trigger Q1 Volume

Why tiered SLAs? Research consistently shows that teams that automated their way to sub-15-minute response times exceeded quota by 23%, while those averaging 4-hour responses hit only 12%. But not every event lead warrants the same urgency. Tiering by account value and engagement signal ensures AEs prioritize the highest-potential leads while marketing catches the rest automatically.

Step 2: Build the Automation Layer

T+0 (At Event)

Capture & Enrich

Event check-in (badge scan / host confirmation) triggers Marketo program status update to "Attended." Workato workflow enriches record with account tier, AE owner, and existing opp data from Salesforce.

T+0 to T+4 hours

Hot Lead Routing

For üî¥ Hot leads: Workato creates SFDC task assigned to AE Owner with subject "[Event Name] - Follow Up: [Contact Name] - Tier 1 Attendee." Due date = today. Slack DM sent to AE with meeting context notes.

T+24 hours

Warm Lead Routing + First Nurture Email

For üü° Warm leads: SFDC task created for AE with 48-hour due date. Simultaneously, Marketo sends personalized "Great meeting you at [Event]" email with a CTA to book a follow-up conversation.

T+72 hours

Escalation Check

Workato checks if üî¥/üü° SFDC tasks are completed. If not ‚Üí escalate to AE manager via Slack. If meeting booked ‚Üí route to normal pipeline process. If not booked ‚Üí continue nurture sequence.

T+5 to T+14 days

Nurture Sequence

For all unboked attendees: 3-touch Marketo email sequence (event recap content ‚Üí case study relevant to their account type ‚Üí direct book-a-meeting CTA). Scoring model adds +15 points for email engagement.

T+14 days

Recycle Decision

If no meeting booked after 14 days: auto-move to long-term nurture with disposition reason code. AE must log a reason for not converting (wrong contact, not in buying cycle, disqualified, etc.). These codes feed back to marketing for targeting refinement.
